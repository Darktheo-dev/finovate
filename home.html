Flight Profile Explained:

1. The Rocket's Push (Thrust)

Thrust goes **UP** at first, then **DOWN**. The initial increase happens because rocket engines are more powerful in thin air. The later decrease is a deliberate safety maneuver to survive **Max-Q** (peak air pressure) and to manage acceleration ($a = F/m$) as the rocket gets lighter from burning fuel ($m$).

2. The Fuel Plan (Propellant)

The simulation compares the **Actual Propellant** (solid line) to a perfect flight plan, known as a **"Digital Twin"** (dotted line). A key task is to watch for a **deviation**, where the solid line drops below the dotted one, indicating a potential problem.

3. The Results (Flight Dynamics)

This graph shows the rocket's **Altitude** and **Velocity**, which are calculated from the total upward force (Thrust minus Gravity) and the rocket's changing mass. Watch how quickly the velocity climbs as the rocket gets lighter.
